# God Meets Us in the Wait

## Ages 6–10 — Learning to Be Still Before God

Sometimes, waiting feels hard.

Children don’t always like to wait. Grown-ups don’t either. Waiting can feel quiet, slow, and uncomfortable.

But waiting can also be a gift.

Before going into church, families often stop on the steps outside. The noise slows down. Feet stop moving. Voices soften. Hearts begin to settle.

That quiet moment is a kind of waiting.

The Bible tells us that God is near when we slow down and wait for Him. God doesn’t always speak loudly. Sometimes He speaks in stillness.

When we sit quietly, pray softly, or listen instead of rushing, God meets us there.

Waiting doesn’t mean nothing is happening. It means God is helping our hearts get ready.

### Simple truth for kids (6–10)
When we slow down and wait, God is closer than we think.
