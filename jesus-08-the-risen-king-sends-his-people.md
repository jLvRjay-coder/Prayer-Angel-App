# Episode 8 — The Risen King Sends His People

---

## Ages 6–9 — The King Gives a Mission

### 🧭 Big Idea
The risen King gives His followers a mission — and He stays with them.

### 📜 The Story
After Jesus rose from the dead, He did not leave right away.

He met with His disciples.

They had been afraid.
They had hidden behind locked doors.
They had watched Him die.

Now He stood alive in front of them.

He showed them His hands.
He spoke peace over them.
He ate with them.

This was not a dream.

It was the risen King.

One day, Jesus gathered them and said something important:

“Go ye therefore, and teach all nations.”

He was giving them a mission.

They were not soldiers.
They were not famous leaders.

They were fishermen and ordinary men.

But they had seen the empty tomb.
They had seen the risen King.

And Jesus made a promise:

“Lo, I am with you alway.”

He was sending them out.

But He was not leaving them alone.

The story was not ending.

It was beginning again.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Jesus is alive and still leading.
- He gives His followers important work to do.
- We are never alone in His mission.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why do you think Jesus gave His friends a job to do?
> - What does it mean that He is always with us?

## Ages 10–13 — Authority Shared, Not Hoarded

### 🧭 Big Idea
Because Jesus has all authority, His followers can obey boldly.

### 📜 The Story
Before Jesus returned to heaven, He gathered His followers one more time.

These were not powerful rulers.

They were men who had once argued about greatness.
Men who had run away when He was arrested.
Men who had doubted and feared.

Yet He said to them:

“All power is given unto me in heaven and in earth.”

That was a royal claim.

Not partial authority.
Not temporary influence.

All authority.

Then He said, “Go therefore…”

The mission flowed from His kingship.

Because He reigns,
they go.

He did not tell them to overthrow Rome.

He told them to make disciples.
To teach.
To baptize.

“All nations.”

The kingdom would not grow by force.

It would grow by truth.

Then He made a promise that changed everything:

“Lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world.”

The King did not just give commands.

He gave presence.

Their courage would not come from confidence in themselves.

It would come from confidence in Him.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- The resurrection empowers mission.
- Christ’s authority fuels obedience.
- The kingdom expands through discipleship, not violence.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why is it important that Jesus said He has “all authority”?
> - How does His presence give courage?
> - Where might He be calling you to obey?

## Young Adult / Adult — Delegated Reign

### 🧭 Big Idea
The Great Commission is the first decree of the enthroned King.

### 📜 The Story
The Great Commission is not inspirational suggestion.

It is royal mandate.

“All authority in heaven and in earth.”

The scope is cosmic.

This is not a regional king extending influence.
This is the resurrected Son claiming universal reign.

And then — astonishingly — He sends others.

Throughout Israel’s history, kings centralized power.

Authority was guarded, protected, hoarded.

Christ does the opposite.

He delegates.

The disciples who once fled now stand commissioned.
The ones who misunderstood now carry proclamation.
The fearful are entrusted with global mission.

This is not human confidence restored.

It is divine authority entrusted.

The command is simple:

“Go.”

The verb assumes motion.
It assumes risk.
It assumes obedience.

The kingdom does not advance by territorial conquest.
It advances by transformed lives.

It does not depend on political leverage.
It depends on gospel proclamation.

The resurrection settled identity.

The commission establishes direction.

The ascension does not remove Christ’s presence.

It multiplies it.

“I am with you alway.”

The enthroned King is not distant.

He reigns — and He remains.

This is the hinge into the Church.

The throne is occupied.
The Spirit will empower.
The mission will expand.

Not by force.

By witness.

The reign of Christ now advances through the obedience of His people.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Christ’s authority is comprehensive and present.
- Mission flows from kingship, not emotion.
- The Church exists because the King reigns.
- Obedience is allegiance made visible.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - If all authority belongs to Christ, how should that shape your daily decisions?
> - Where are you hesitant to go?
> - What would bold obedience look like in your context?
