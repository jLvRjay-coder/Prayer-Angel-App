# Getting Ready for God

## A simple lesson about preparing our hearts

### The Steps Before Church

Every Sunday morning, the steps outside our church filled with families. Kids laughed, adults talked, and shoes tapped against the concrete as everyone made their way inside.

But before we ever opened the church doors, my mama had a rule.

“You don’t just walk into God’s house,” she’d say.  
“You get your heart ready first.”

Sometimes that meant fixing our attitude.  
Sometimes it meant saying sorry to a sibling.  
Sometimes it meant standing still and being quiet for just a moment.

Those church steps became a special place — a pause between the noise of the world and the peace inside.

### Learning to Be Still

As a child, I didn’t always understand why it mattered. I just knew that something felt different when we stopped and prepared our hearts.

I learned that God cares about **how** we come to Him — not just that we come.

When my heart was calm, I listened better.  
When my heart was humble, I learned more.  
When my heart was ready, God spoke.

### What God Was Teaching Me

God doesn’t rush us.  
He invites us.

He asks us to slow down, to come honestly, and to remember who He is before we speak.

Those steps taught me something I still carry today:

**Meeting with God starts in the heart.**
