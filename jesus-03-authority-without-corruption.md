# Episode 3 — Authority Without Corruption

---

## Ages 6–9 — A King Who Chose What Was Right

### 🧭 Big Idea
Real strength chooses obedience, even when shortcuts look easier.

### 📜 The Story
Before Jesus began teaching large crowds, something important happened.

He went into the wilderness.

He was alone.
He was hungry.
He had not eaten for many days.

That is when the devil came to tempt Him.

“If You are the Son of God,” the tempter said, “turn these stones into bread.”

Jesus was hungry. He had the power to do it.

But He answered, “Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God.”

Then the devil tempted Him again.

He showed Jesus all the kingdoms of the world and said, “All this power will I give You, if You will fall down and worship me.”

It sounded like an easy way to become King.

No suffering.
No waiting.
No obedience.

But Jesus said no.

He would not take power the wrong way.
He would not bow to evil.
He would not choose a shortcut.

After the wilderness, Jesus returned and began to teach.

People were amazed.

He did not speak like other teachers.
He spoke with authority.

Even unclean spirits obeyed Him.

This was a King with real power.

But He always used it the right way.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Real strength obeys God.
- Power should never be used the wrong way.
- Shortcuts can lead us away from what is right.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why do you think Jesus refused the easy way?
> - What does it mean to use strength wisely?

## Ages 10–13 — Tested Before Trusted

### 🧭 Big Idea
Authority that lasts is built on obedience first.

### 📜 The Story
Before Jesus healed the sick or calmed storms, He faced temptation.

Forty days in the wilderness.

Hungry.
Alone.
Weakened physically.

The temptations were not random.

They targeted who He was and how He would rule.

“If You are the Son of God…”

Prove it.
Use Your power for Yourself.
Take control now.

The first temptation was about appetite.

Turn stones into bread.

The second was about spectacle.

Throw Yourself down so angels must rescue You.

The third was about authority.

Bow down, and all the kingdoms of the world can be Yours.

No cross.
No suffering.
No waiting.

Each offer promised influence without obedience.

But Jesus answered every temptation with Scripture.

He refused to manipulate power for personal comfort.
He rejected public spectacle.
He declined political dominance through compromise.

Where Israel had failed in the wilderness long ago, Jesus remained faithful.

Where Adam failed in a garden, Jesus stood firm in a desert.

When He left the wilderness, His authority became visible.

He taught differently.
He commanded spirits.
He healed the sick.

And people noticed.

Not because He demanded attention.

But because integrity carries weight.

His authority was clean.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Character is tested before influence is trusted.
- Temptation often attacks identity and timing.
- Obedience strengthens leadership.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why was it important that Jesus faced temptation?
> - How can resisting shortcuts shape your character?
> - Where are you tempted to prove yourself too quickly?

## Young Adult / Adult — The Nature of Uncorrupted Power

### 🧭 Big Idea
Christ’s authority was proven in submission before it was revealed in power.

### 📜 The Story
The wilderness was not merely a test of endurance.

It was a confrontation over kingship.

Immediately after His baptism — where the Father declared, “This is my beloved Son” — Jesus was led into isolation.

Before public ministry came private testing.

The adversary’s strategy was precise.

Each temptation offered a version of power detached from obedience.

Provision without dependence.
Recognition without humility.
Dominion without submission.

Turn stones into bread — meet legitimate needs apart from the Father’s timing.

Throw Yourself down — manufacture visible proof instead of trusting quiet identity.

Bow down — claim global authority without the suffering required to redeem it.

Each shortcut bypassed the cross.

Each invitation preserved comfort.

Each path separated authority from surrender.

Jesus refused every one.

He would not weaponize His divinity for personal preservation.
He would not seize a crown before obedience was complete.
He would not rule apart from the Father’s will.

This is where His kingship diverges from every earthly throne.

Saul grasped for control.
Ahab manipulated outcomes.
Herod protected position through fear.
Rome enforced authority through force.

Christ submitted.

His authority was not seized.

It was received.

Because it was rooted in obedience, it carried a weight no empire could manufacture.

When He entered synagogues, demons recognized Him before crowds did.

When He spoke, storms obeyed.

When He forgave sins, religious leaders trembled.

Authority without corruption is rare because human power often bends inward.

The wilderness reveals that Jesus’ power would never serve self-protection.

It would serve redemption.

Earthly authority climbs.

Divine authority kneels — and through kneeling, conquers.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Temptation reveals how we intend to use influence.
- Authority separated from obedience becomes corruption.
- Submission to God is the foundation of lasting leadership.
- Christ’s power was exercised for redemption, not preservation.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Where are you tempted to separate success from obedience?
> - What shortcuts seem justified in your current season?
> - How would your influence change if rooted fully in submission to God?
