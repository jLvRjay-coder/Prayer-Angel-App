Waiting is hard.

For kids ages 9–13, waiting can feel even harder because life already feels full of questions. School pressures. Friend issues. Family changes. Big feelings with no clear answers.

Sometimes it feels like God is quiet during those moments.

But quiet does not mean absent.

In the Bible, God often met His people while they waited, not after the waiting was over. David waited years before becoming king. Joseph waited in prison before freedom came. Even Jesus waited before beginning His ministry.

Waiting was never wasted.

Being still before God doesn’t mean doing nothing. It means choosing trust when action feels impossible. It means praying even when words feel small. It means staying faithful when answers don’t come right away.

God meets us in the waiting places — the moments where patience is stretched and faith is tested. He listens when we feel alone, even when the answer takes time.

Waiting teaches us something important: God works on our hearts while we wait on His timing.

And when God does move, we often realize He was present the whole time.
