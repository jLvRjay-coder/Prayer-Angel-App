# Episode 3 — When Desire Turns Into Injustice

## Ages 6–9 — A Vineyard That Wasn’t for Sale

There was a man named Naboth who owned a vineyard.

It was not just a garden. It had been in his family for a long time, passed down from one generation to the next. God’s law said that land like this should stay with the family  
(1 Kings 21:1–3, KJV).

One day, King Ahab saw Naboth’s vineyard and wanted it.

He asked Naboth to sell it or trade it for something better. But Naboth said no. He wanted to obey God and protect what had been given to his family.

Ahab went home angry and sad. He lay on his bed and refused to eat.

Jezebel saw him and said she would get the vineyard for him.

But she did not choose a good or fair way.

### Simple truth for kids (6–9)
Just because we want something doesn’t mean we should take it. God cares about what is fair.

---

## Ages 10–13 — When Wanting Becomes Wrong

Naboth’s vineyard was close to the palace, and King Ahab wanted it badly.

When Naboth refused to sell, Ahab became upset instead of respecting Naboth’s obedience to God  
(1 Kings 21:4).

Jezebel stepped in and took control.

She used Ahab’s name and authority to write letters and accuse Naboth of things he did not do. She planned it carefully so it looked legal, even though it was wrong  
(1 Kings 21:8–10).

Naboth was punished for something he never did, and Ahab took the vineyard.

This story shows how desire can turn into injustice when power is misused.

### Reflection for 10–13
Why is it wrong to use power to get what we want?

---

## Young Adult / Adult — Injustice Hidden Behind Authority

Naboth’s vineyard represented inheritance, obedience, and God’s design for justice.

When Ahab wanted what was not his, he allowed Jezebel to act in his name. Scripture records how false witnesses were used and Naboth was condemned and killed  
(1 Kings 21:8–13).

This was not an accident or a moment of anger. It was calculated injustice.

Systems meant to protect the innocent were manipulated, and Ahab accepted the benefit without protest. Desire replaced righteousness, and authority was used to destroy.

God sees injustice, even when it is hidden behind power and position.

### Reflection for young adults / adults
Where might desire be tempting you to justify what God clearly calls wrong?
