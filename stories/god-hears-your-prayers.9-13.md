# 📖 God Hears Your Prayers

*A testimony of childlike faith and the faithfulness of God.*

## Growing Up With a Heart for God

When I was young, something in me was drawn to the things of God. I was bold, curious, and eager to understand who Jesus is and why church matters. Even though no one in my home attended, I felt a pull—so I started going on my own.

I joined GA’s, Bible School, and anything else my little church offered. I soaked up every word, every song, and every Scripture. But even with all that involvement, I carried a quiet ache:

**I wanted my family to know God too.**

At nine years old, I surrendered my life to Jesus. It was real. It was personal. It changed me. And with that new life came an even deeper longing for my family.

## A Little Prayer Closet

To find peace, I created my own “prayer closet”—a small, hidden space where I could kneel, whisper prayers, and pour out my heart to God. If that old home were still standing today, I could walk you right to the exact spot where my knees touched the floor.

In that little place, I prayed the same prayer again and again:

> “Lord, please lead Mom and Dad to church…  
> and let them take me—not send me.  
> And God… please call my dad to preach.”

My parents were good, loving, moral people. I had never seen either of them drink or live in ways that dishonored the Lord. They were kind and hardworking. But I wanted them to know Jesus—not just as a name—but as Savior.

## And Then God Moved

Then something happened that I’ll never forget.

At ten years old, I sat in church and watched—eyes wide, heart pounding—as my dad was **ordained as a Baptist minister.** The prayer I had whispered in secret… God brought to life right in front of me.

That day, my parents walked into church as ordinary attendees.  
And they walked out as **answered prayer.**

## A Final Encouragement

Whether you are **9 or 90**, God still hears.  
He still answers.  
He still moves in ways far beyond what we can see.

Keep praying.  
Keep trusting.  
Keep believing.

His Word hasn’t changed—and His faithfulness never will.

4. Who needs your steady, faithful prayers right now?
5. Looking back, can you recognize a moment when God answered something you once prayed in private?
