# The Death of King Saul (Adult)

Saul’s ending was not sudden.
It was the result of a long pattern.

He began as a humble man hiding among the baggage.
But over time, insecurity replaced trust.
Control replaced obedience.
Fear replaced faith.

Saul stopped listening to God.

When David had the chance to end Saul’s life, he refused (1 Samuel 24).
David understood something Saul did not: leadership belongs to God.
Only God raises up and removes kings.

Saul’s downfall was not David’s victory.
It was the consequence of a hardened heart.

By the time Saul died in battle (1 Samuel 31), the tragedy was complete.
But David did not celebrate.

He mourned.

David grieved for the man who tried to kill him.
He honored the position Saul once held.
He trusted that justice belonged to the Lord.

Saul’s story warns us:
Small compromises harden the heart.
Unchecked jealousy grows into destruction.
Disobedience compounds over time.

David’s response teaches us:
Revenge delays God’s work.
Trust accelerates it.

Leadership is not preserved by force.
It is preserved by obedience.

### What This Teaches
- Pride and insecurity can quietly destroy what God once blessed.
- Revenge is a temptation that weakens integrity.
- God alone establishes and removes authority.
- Obedience protects your future.

## Think About It
1) Is there any area where pride or fear is shaping your decisions?
2) Where are you tempted to take control instead of trusting God?
3) What would it look like to leave justice in God’s hands?
