# David Anointed King (Adult)

David’s story reminds us that God’s calling often comes long before the crown.

When Samuel anointed David as a young shepherd (1 Samuel 16), the promise was real — but the fulfillment was distant. David would spend years serving, hiding, fleeing, and learning before he ever sat on the throne.

He was chosen early. He was crowned later.

Between those two moments was a long stretch of waiting.

David did not seize power. He did not manipulate outcomes. He refused to kill Saul when given the opportunity. Instead, he trusted that if God had promised the throne, God would deliver it in His time.

When David was finally anointed king over all Israel (2 Samuel 5), it was not sudden success. It was the visible outcome of hidden faithfulness.

God’s promises are never rushed.
They unfold through obedience, humility, and trust.

David teaches us that waiting is not wasted.
It is preparation.

### What This Teaches
- God often prepares us in private before promoting us in public.
- Waiting refines character.
- Obedience during delay is proof of trust.
- God’s timing is precise — never early, never late.

## Think About It
1) What promise of God are you currently waiting to see fulfilled?
2) Where might God be shaping your character in hidden seasons?
3) Are you trusting God’s timing — or trying to control the outcome?
