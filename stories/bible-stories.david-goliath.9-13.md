# David and Goliath (Adult)

David’s story is not a “feel-good” tale about a brave young man beating impossible odds. It is a declaration that God is King, and that the outcome of every battle ultimately belongs to Him.

Israel had soldiers. They had armor. They had experience. But fear ruled the camp because Goliath didn’t just threaten Israel — he defied the living God.

David came from the sheepfold, not the battlefield. Yet his confidence was not in personality, optimism, or courage as a trait. It was grounded in a simple conviction: God had been faithful before — and He would be faithful again.

When David said, “I come to thee in the name of the LORD of hosts” (1 Samuel 17:45), he was making the real issue clear: this was not David versus Goliath — it was about the honor of God.

David did not deny the danger. He simply refused to treat the giant as the greatest reality in front of him.

One stone. One moment. And God reminded everyone watching that strength is never the foundation — faith is.


## What This Teaches

- Fear shrinks when God becomes bigger in our view.
- Faith does not deny reality — it submits reality to God’s authority.
- God is glorified when His people trust Him instead of the arm of flesh.


## Think About It

1. What “giant” is shaping your decisions right now — fear, pressure, insecurity, temptation, or exhaustion?
2. Where have you been relying on “armor” (control, image, money, approval) instead of trusting the Lord?
3. What would it look like to face this week in the name of the Lord, with obedience and faith?
