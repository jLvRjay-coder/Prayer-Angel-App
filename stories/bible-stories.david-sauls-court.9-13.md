# David in King Saul’s Court (Ages 9–13)

David’s victory over Goliath opened a new season: service in King Saul’s court.

David served faithfully.
He played music when Saul was troubled, and God used it to bring calm.

As David succeeded, people began to praise him.
That praise planted something dangerous in Saul’s heart: jealousy.

Saul’s problem wasn’t really David — it was fear, control, and a refusal to trust God.
Jealousy turned into anger, and anger turned into choices that hurt people.

David had opportunities to respond with pride or revenge.
Instead, David stayed humble.
He kept serving.
He trusted God with the outcome.

### What This Teaches
- Success can bring new tests, not fewer.
- Jealousy grows when we stop trusting God.
- Humility protects your heart when others act unfairly.

## Reflection (9–13)
1) How do you respond when someone treats you unfairly?
2) What does humility look like when you’re being misunderstood?
3) Where might God be teaching you patience right now?
