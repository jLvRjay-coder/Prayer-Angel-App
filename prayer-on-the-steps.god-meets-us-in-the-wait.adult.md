# God Meets Us in the Wait

## Adult — Trust Formed Between Prayer and Answer

The church steps have a way of slowing us down.

They sit between the noise of the world and the stillness of worship — a place where time feels exposed. For many adults, this is where waiting becomes unavoidable.

We pray.  
We ask.  
We trust.  

And then nothing moves.

Scripture never treats waiting as wasted time. Again and again, God forms His people *in the pause*. David waited years between anointing and kingship. Abraham waited decades for the promise. Even Jesus waited before His public ministry began.

Waiting is not God withholding goodness — it is God shaping readiness.

Psalm 27:14 commands rather than comforts:  
“Wait on the LORD: be of good courage, and he shall strengthen thine heart” (KJV).

Strength comes **during** the wait, not after it.

The steps remind us that prayer is not a transaction. It is a relationship. God is present even when answers feel delayed, and His silence is never absence.

The hardest prayers are often answered not with change in circumstances, but with change in us.

### Reflection for adults
What might God be forming in you during the season you want to rush past?
