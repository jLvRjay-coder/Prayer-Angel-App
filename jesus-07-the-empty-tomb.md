# Episode 7 — The Empty Tomb

---

## Ages 6–9 — The Stone Was Rolled Away

### 🧭 Big Idea
Jesus is alive — and death did not win.

### 📜 The Story
After Jesus died, His friends placed His body in a tomb.

It was carved into rock.
A large stone was rolled in front of the entrance.

Soldiers stood guard.

It felt finished.
It felt final.

The disciples were afraid.
The crowds were quiet.

Early on the third day, while it was still dark, some women walked toward the tomb.

They carried spices.
They expected to find His body.

They wondered, “Who will roll away the stone?”

But when they arrived, they stopped.

The stone had already been moved.

The tomb was open.

An angel stood there and said,  
“Fear not… He is not here: for he is risen.”

They looked inside.

The tomb was empty.

Jesus was alive.

Death had tried to hold Him.

It could not.

The King had defeated it.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Jesus did not stay in the grave.
- Death could not defeat God’s power.
- The resurrection gives real hope.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why do you think the empty tomb surprised everyone?
> - What does it mean that Jesus is alive today?

## Ages 10–13 — Proof That Changes Everything

### 🧭 Big Idea
The resurrection proves that Jesus is truly King.

### 📜 The Story
The resurrection was not a rumor started in secret.

The tomb had been sealed.
Roman guards were posted.
Many witnesses had seen Jesus die.

His body was wrapped and laid inside the grave.

It seemed certain.

On the third day, everything changed.

The stone was rolled away — not to let Jesus out, but to let witnesses in.

Angels announced it.
Women discovered it.
Peter and John ran to see it for themselves.

The grave clothes were still there.
The body was gone.

Later, Jesus appeared to His disciples.

He spoke with them.
He ate with them.
He showed them the wounds in His hands.

This was not imagination.
Not a dream.
Not a legend growing over time.

It was physical resurrection.

If Jesus had remained in the grave, His teachings would have ended as tragedy.

But because He rose, everything He said was confirmed.

The cross paid the price.

The resurrection proved it was accepted.

Death is powerful.

But it is not final.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- The resurrection confirms Jesus’ identity.
- Death does not have the final word.
- Christian hope is grounded in a real event.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why would the disciples risk their lives for something untrue?
> - How does knowing Jesus rose from the grave give you courage?
> - What fears change if death is not ultimate?

## Young Adult / Adult — The Defeat of Finality

### 🧭 Big Idea
The resurrection is the irreversible victory of the true King.

### 📜 The Story
The resurrection is not symbolic encouragement.

It is historical confrontation.

Rome excelled at execution.
Crucifixion ensured death.
A sealed tomb guaranteed finality.

In the ancient world, death was the boundary no one crossed.

Kings died.
Prophets died.
Empires buried their heroes and moved on.

The Son of God entered that boundary deliberately.

The cross satisfied justice.

The resurrection declared victory.

If Christ remained dead, the movement collapses.  
Paul states plainly, “If Christ be not risen, your faith is vain.”

The empty tomb is divine vindication.

The Father’s public declaration that the sacrifice was accepted.

That sin was paid for.
That judgment was absorbed.
That the curse was broken.

The stone was not rolled away to free Christ.

It was rolled away to confront the world.

Death, which had reigned since Eden, had met One it could not contain.

Christ did not merely survive death.

He exhausted it.

The resurrection does not erase suffering.

It transforms its meaning.

It declares that injustice is not ultimate.
Graves are not permanent.
Loss does not write the final sentence.

No empire can outlast death.

But Christ has.

The resurrection is not the end of the story.

It is the beginning of new creation.

The King who was crucified now lives.

His reign cannot be buried.
His authority cannot decay.
His kingdom cannot be sealed shut.

Allegiance to Him is not nostalgia for a fallen hero.

It is loyalty to a living King.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- The resurrection validates the cross.
- Death is defeated, not merely delayed.
- Christ’s kingdom cannot be extinguished.
- Christian hope rests on historical resurrection.

> - Becaiuse of the resurrection, what changes in your life?
> - Where does fear of death still influence you?
> - How should a risen King reshape your confidence and priorities?
