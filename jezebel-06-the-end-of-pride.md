# Episode 6 — The End of Pride

---

## Ages 6–9 — God’s Warnings Were Ignored

### 🧭 Big Idea
God is patient, but pride cannot stand forever.

### 📜 The Story
Jezebel had been warned many times.

Prophets had spoken God’s truth.
Miracles had shown His power.
Mercy had given her time to change.

But Jezebel would not listen.

Years passed.

Then a new leader named Jehu came toward the city. God had chosen him to bring judgment.

When Jezebel heard he was coming, she did something surprising.

She painted her eyes.
She fixed her hair.
She stood proudly at the window  
(2 Kings 9:30–31, KJV).

She did not look afraid.

She spoke boldly, as if her position would protect her.

But no palace wall can block God’s word.

Jehu commanded, and servants threw her down from the window  
(2 Kings 9:33, KJV).

Her life ended just as God had warned years before.

God had been patient.

But His truth stood firm.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- God gives warnings because He cares.
- Pride refuses to listen.
- God is patient, but He always does what is right.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why is it important to listen to warnings?
> - What can happen when pride keeps us from changing?

## Ages 10–13 — Pride Has Consequences

### 🧭 Big Idea
Ignoring truth does not stop it from coming true.

### 📜 The Story
Jezebel knew who Jehu was.

She knew why he had come.

Years earlier, Elijah had declared that judgment would fall on her because of her evil and cruelty  
(1 Kings 21:23, KJV).

Now that day had arrived.

Instead of humbling herself, she prepared herself like a queen.

She stood at the window and mocked Jehu  
(2 Kings 9:30–31, KJV).

Her pride had not softened with time.

It had hardened.

Jehu looked up and called out, asking who would stand with him.

Servants stepped forward.

At his command, they threw her down.

Her death fulfilled exactly what God had spoken  
(2 Kings 9:33–36, KJV).

This was not sudden anger.

It was long-declared judgment.

Every warning had been real.

Every prophecy had been true.

Pride had ignored mercy.

But mercy misunderstood is not protection.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- Pride can blind us to truth.
- God’s patience does not mean permission.
- Warnings are gifts meant to lead us back.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why do people ignore warnings?
> - How can pride keep someone from changing?
> - What happens when mercy is mistaken for safety?

## Young Adult / Adult — Judgment Fulfilled Exactly as Spoken

### 🧭 Big Idea
God’s patience is vast — but His justice is precise.

### 📜 The Story
Jezebel’s final scene is sobering.

For years she had shaped policy, silenced prophets, orchestrated injustice, and led Israel deeper into idolatry. She survived drought, confrontation, and delayed judgment.

Each year of survival may have felt like validation.

It was mercy.

When Jehu approached Jezreel, the prophetic clock struck.

She adorned herself and stood at the window  
(2 Kings 9:30, KJV).

The gesture is telling.

Not repentance.
Not trembling.

Presentation.

Power had long been her shield.

But prophetic words do not decay with time.

Jehu lifted his face and asked, “Who is on my side?”

Servants answered.

At his command, she was thrown down  
(2 Kings 9:33–37, KJV).

The judgment unfolded exactly as Elijah had declared decades earlier  
(1 Kings 21:23, KJV).

No embellishment.
No exaggeration.

Exact fulfillment.

This account is not written to satisfy curiosity about violence.

It is written to confront the illusion of immunity.

Jezebel believed position insulated her.
Time would dilute prophecy.
Strength would outlast warning.

But covenant justice is not erased by delay.

God’s patience had extended opportunity.

Her refusal had solidified outcome.

There were no recorded tears.
No late humility.
No final plea.

The arc closes without redemption — not because mercy was withheld, but because it was resisted.

Her name becomes more than biography.

It becomes warning.

Pride may ascend balconies.

It cannot escape judgment.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- God’s warnings are acts of mercy.
- Delay in judgment is not cancellation.
- Pride resists repentance until consequences arrive.
- Divine justice fulfills precisely what God has spoken.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Where have you mistaken delay for approval?
> - What warnings from God are easy to ignore because consequences seem distant?
> - What would humility look like before consequences arrive?
