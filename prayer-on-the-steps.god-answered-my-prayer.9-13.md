# God Answered My Prayer

Faith doesn’t always shout. Sometimes it waits quietly and trusts God anyway.

## Growing Up With a Heart for God

From the time I was young, something in me was drawn to God. I was curious, bold, and eager to understand who Jesus was and why church mattered. Even though no one in my home attended church, I felt a pull to go — so I went on my own.

I joined GA’s, Bible School, and anything else my small church offered. I listened closely, sang every song, and tried to understand every Scripture. Still, a quiet ache stayed with me. I wanted my family to know God too.

When I was nine years old, I gave my heart to Jesus. It was real. It was personal. And it changed me.

But one prayer stayed constant.

## A Little Prayer Closet

I made my own prayer closet — a small, hidden space where I could kneel and talk to God. If that house still stood today, I could walk you to the exact spot where my knees touched the floor.

Over and over, I prayed the same prayer:

“Lord, please lead Mom and Dad to church.  
Please let them take me — not send me.  
And God… please call my dad to preach.”

My parents were kind, loving, and hardworking people. I had never seen them drink or live in ways that dishonored God. But I wanted them to know Jesus the way I did.

## Watching God Move

One day, when I was ten years old, I sat in church and watched something I will never forget.

My daddy was ordained as a Baptist minister.

The prayer I had whispered in secret — the one I prayed alone — God answered right in front of me.

That day, my parents walked into church as attendees.  
They walked out as an answered prayer.

## What I Learned About Prayer

God hears every prayer — even the ones prayed quietly by a child.

Sometimes answers come quickly.  
Sometimes they take time.  
But God never forgets the cries of His children.

Keep praying.  
Keep trusting.  
Keep believing.

God is faithful — always.
