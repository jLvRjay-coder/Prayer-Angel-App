# When God Answers in His Time

## Ages 9–13 — Trusting God When You Don’t See It Yet

Waiting is hard—especially when you’re old enough to notice what you *want* and how long it’s taking.

Sometimes you pray and nothing changes.  
Your situation stays the same.  
The pressure doesn’t go away.  
And it can feel like God is quiet.

But quiet doesn’t mean God is absent.

In Scripture, God often worked *while* people waited.  
Joseph waited in places he didn’t choose.  
David waited years before he became king.  
Even Jesus waited until the “right time” to begin His ministry.

God’s timing is not slow. It’s purposeful.

Sometimes God answers by changing the situation.  
Sometimes He answers by strengthening *you*.  
And sometimes His answer is, “Not yet”—because He’s preparing something you can’t see.

On the steps before church, waiting looks like slowing down.  
It looks like choosing peace on purpose.  
It looks like trusting that God is working even when you can’t measure it.

If you’re waiting on something right now, don’t quit praying.  
Don’t quit believing.  
And don’t confuse God’s timing with God’s love.

He hears you.  
He sees you.  
And He answers in His time.
