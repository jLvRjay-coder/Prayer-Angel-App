# Episode 3 — The Jar That Did Not Run Dry

---

## Ages 6–9 — A Little Oil, A Big Miracle

### 🧭 Big Idea
God can provide even when we think there is not enough.

### 📜 The Story
The brook where Elijah had been staying dried up.

So God told him to go to a town called Zarephath  
(1 Kings 17:8–9, KJV).

When Elijah arrived, he saw a widow gathering sticks.

She looked tired.

Elijah asked her for some water and a piece of bread.

She answered honestly.

“I have only a handful of meal in a barrel, and a little oil in a cruse. I am gathering two sticks, that I may go in and dress it for me and my son, that we may eat it, and die.”  
(1 Kings 17:12, KJV)

She thought this would be their last meal.

Elijah said something surprising:

“Fear not… but make me thereof a little cake first.”  
(1 Kings 17:13, KJV)

That sounded hard.

But then he gave her a promise from God:

“The barrel of meal shall not waste, neither shall the cruse of oil fail.”  
(1 Kings 17:14, KJV)

The widow chose to trust.

She baked the bread.

And just as God had said, the flour did not run out.

The oil did not run dry.

Day after day, there was enough.

Not extra.
Not overflowing.

But enough.

God multiplied what seemed too small.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- God sees people in hard places.
- Trust sometimes means giving first.
- God can make small things last.
- Obedience opens the door to provision.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why was it hard for the widow to share?
> - What does it mean to trust God?
> - Have you ever seen God provide in a small but special way?

## Ages 10–13 — Faith in the Middle of Scarcity

### 🧭 Big Idea
Faith often looks like obedience before results.

### 📜 The Story
Zarephath was not in Israel.

It was in Sidon — the homeland of Jezebel  
(1 Kings 17:9, KJV).

God sent His prophet outside Israel’s borders to a Gentile widow.

That alone is remarkable.

The widow had almost nothing left.

One handful of flour.
A little oil.
One final meal planned.

Elijah’s request tested her faith.

“Give to God first.”

He promised that the meal would not run out and the oil would not fail.

This was not manipulation.

It was invitation.

If she refused, she would eat once and die.

If she trusted, she would depend daily.

She chose trust.

And the miracle was steady, not dramatic.

The jar did not overflow.
The oil did not multiply into rivers.

It simply did not run out.

Day after day.

The drought still covered the land.

Circumstances did not disappear.

But God sustained her through them.

Provision sometimes looks like preservation.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- God’s care extends beyond borders.
- Faith requires action.
- Obedience often comes before visible blessing.
- God provides enough for the day.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - Why do you think God sent Elijah outside Israel?
> - What would you have done in the widow’s place?
> - What does daily faith look like for you?

## Young Adult / Adult — The Discipline of Daily Provision

### 🧭 Big Idea
God’s provision often sustains rather than removes pressure.

### 📜 The Story
1 Kings 17 shifts the stage.

Elijah moves from hidden brook to foreign soil.

God directs him not to a priest, not to a wealthy patron, but to a marginalized widow in Sidon — the very region associated with Baal worship.

The contrast is deliberate.

The God of Israel provides in Baal’s homeland.

The widow stands at the edge of despair.

Her language is honest.
Her expectation is minimal.
Her theology is survival-based.

Elijah’s command confronts scarcity thinking:

“Make me thereof a little cake first.”

It echoes a consistent biblical pattern:

Trust precedes multiplication.

The miracle itself is understated.

The jar does not overflow.
The oil does not visibly expand.

It simply continues.

The continuity is the miracle.

Daily sufficiency replaces visible abundance.

Notice what does not happen:

The drought does not end.
Economic conditions do not immediately improve.
Comfort is not restored.

God demonstrates faithfulness by sustaining through pressure — not removing it.

Scarcity exposes belief.

The widow’s obedience preserves her household.
Her trust becomes testimony.
Elijah’s dependence deepens before public confrontation at Carmel.

Formation continues.

Before fire falls from heaven,
faith learns to survive in kitchens.

### 💡 What This Teaches
- God’s provision is often daily, not dramatic.
- Trust must precede visible multiplication.
- Scarcity can refine faith rather than destroy it.
- God’s covenant faithfulness transcends geography and expectation.

> ### 🪑 Sit With This
> - What scarcity is shaping your decisions right now?
> - Are you waiting for abundance before obeying?
> - Where has God quietly sustained you instead of dramatically rescuing you?
